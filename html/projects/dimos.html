DiMOS

A Digital Menu Ordering System

Overview

The Digital Menu Ordering System project aims to revolutionize the order management process in small restaurants. By developing a user-friendly interface and incorporating real-time updates, the system seeks to replace traditional paper tickets, eliminate human errors, and provide data insights for efficient decision-making. This case study presents the project's approach, the achieved results, and the overall conclusion.

Project Background

Restaurants nowadays, especially in large and busy metropolitan areas, need to serve food to large number of people in short time frame, leaving the customer satisfied not only with the quality of the food but also for the quality of the service.

For small businesses is essential to save costs maintaining the same service quality otherwise they cannot survive or compete with large restaurants chains. The aim is to create an affordable application that can enhance the competitiveness of restaurant owners while minimizing costs. DiMOS helps restaurant owners to reduce the workload of their staff and minimize order errors by automating the order-taking process. This eliminates the need for servers to manually write down orders and reduces the likelihood of misheard, misunderstood, or lost orders.

The increased efficiency of the ordering process, facilitated by the app, enables customers to place orders directly from their table, reducing the amount of time servers spend walking back and forth between tables. As a result, restaurants can serve more guests in less time, leading to increased revenue. A well designed and user-friendly web application can enhance the customer experience and satisfaction. DiMOS provides a great customer experience, that can lead to increased customer loyalty and positive word-of-mouth marketing. DiMOS requires only a wireless internet connection and a few touchscreen devices, such as an iPad, tablet, or smartphone, reducing equipment costs and the need for expensive point-of-sale systems or other equipment.

Approach

To create the Kitchen Display System and Digital Menu, a monolithic architecture was adopted with an MVC (Model-View-Controller) design pattern. This design approach helps ensure simplicity in development and deployment while structuring the application in a modular and organized manner.

Model View Controller Approach

The system's frontend was built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with EJS as the templating engine for dynamic content. On the backend, Node.js and Express.js were chosen to develop a scalable and efficient web application.

System Architecture

Role-based access control was implemented to manage user roles, granting specific permissions based on the tasks they needed to perform. The integration of Socket.io facilitated real-time communication, enabling order status updates for both customers and kitchen staff. The project relied on MongoDB Atlas, a cloud-based database, to store data efficiently. Passwords were encrypted using Bcrypt.js to enhance data security.

Results

DiMOS successfully met its objectives. Customers can now browse the digital menu, select their desired meals, and place orders with ease. The kitchen staff can track orders and change orders' status using the Kitchen Display System view. The menu management feature allows kitchen staff to efficiently add, edit, or delete dishes. Real-time updates on order status can make a significant improvement in the order management process. Additionally, the data insights dashboard provides valuable information on popular items, sales trends, and customer preferences.

User feedback during the first release was instrumental in making several improvements to the application. Merging the menu and cart pages, resolving issues with order details links, and enhancing button sizes in the kitchen staff's view improved usability.

Conclusion

The Kitchen Display System and Digital Menu have revolutionized the order management process for small restaurants. By providing an affordable and efficient alternative to traditional systems, the project empowers restaurant owners to digitalize their businesses and increase accuracy, speed, and customer satisfaction. The seamless integration of user-friendly interfaces, real-time updates, and data insights make this system an invaluable asset for small restaurant businesses looking to enhance their operations. With continued improvements and scalability considerations, the project holds the potential to transform the restaurant industry and modernize the dining experience.
