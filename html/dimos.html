DiMOS

A Digital Menu Ordering System

Overview

The Kitchen Display System and Digital Menu project aims to revolutionize the order management process in small restaurants. By developing a user-friendly interface and incorporating real-time updates, the system seeks to replace traditional paper tickets, eliminate human errors, and provide data insights for efficient decision-making. This case study presents the project's approach, the achieved results, and the overall conclusion.

Approach

To create the Kitchen Display System and Digital Menu, a monolithic architecture was adopted to ensure simplicity in development and deployment. The system's frontend was built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with EJS as the templating engine for dynamic content.

On the backend, Node.js and Express.js were chosen to develop a scalable and efficient web application. Role-based access control was implemented to manage user roles, granting specific permissions based on the tasks they needed to perform. The integration of Socket.io facilitated real-time communication, enabling order status updates for both customers and kitchen staff.

The project relied on MongoDB Atlas, a cloud-based database, to store data efficiently. Passwords were encrypted using Bcrypt.js to enhance data security.

Results

The Kitchen Display System and Digital Menu successfully met its objectives. Customers can now browse the digital menu, select their desired meals, and place orders with ease. The menu management feature allows kitchen staff to efficiently add, edit, or delete dishes. Real-time updates on order status have significantly improved the order management process. Additionally, the data insights dashboard provides valuable information on popular items, sales trends, and customer preferences.

User feedback during the first release was instrumental in making several improvements to the application. Merging the menu and cart pages, resolving issues with order details links, and enhancing button sizes in the kitchen staff's view improved usability.

Conclusion

The Kitchen Display System and Digital Menu have revolutionized the order management process for small restaurants. By providing an affordable and efficient alternative to traditional systems, the project empowers restaurant owners to digitalize their businesses and increase accuracy, speed, and customer satisfaction. The seamless integration of user-friendly interfaces, real-time updates, and data insights make this system an invaluable asset for small restaurant businesses looking to enhance their operations. With continued improvements and scalability considerations, the project holds the potential to transform the restaurant industry and modernize the dining experience.
